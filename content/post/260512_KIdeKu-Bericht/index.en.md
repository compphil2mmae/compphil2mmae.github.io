Generative AI not only presents risks but also opportunities for our democratic culture. Our concluding report demonstrates how Large Language Models (LLMs) can be deployed to strengthen public deliberation. First, we identify and describe various use cases for LLMs in deliberative contexts. We then present prototypes and datasets developed in the project: the [KIdeKu Toxicity Detector](https://github.com/debatelab/toxicity-detector) for detecting toxic language, the [EvidenceSeeker Boilerplate](https://debatelab.github.io/evidence-seeker/) for AI-based fact-checking, and the [syncIALO dataset](https://github.com/debatelab/syncIALO) for argumentation. Finally, challenges and recommendations for the use of AI in deliberative contexts are outlined. Particular attention is given to the central role of trust and systematic evaluation.

🔗 The full report is available here: [https://debatelab.github.io/kideku-report/]
